## Objdump -s -j .rdata atom.out | less

>[!note]- Why using objdump -s -j .rdata
> - After getting an initial clue from `strings`, the next natural step is to inspect the section directly  
> - The goal is no longer just “does this string exist?”, but:
>   - where it appears  
>   - what surrounds it  
>   - whether nearby data gives more context  
>
> - That is why I used:
>   ```bash
>   objdump -s -j .rdata atom.out | less
>   ```
>
> - Breakdown:
>   - `-s` → dump section contents (hex + ASCII)  
>   - `-j .rdata` → focus on the section where strings are typically stored  
>   - `| less` → makes it searchable and easier to navigate
