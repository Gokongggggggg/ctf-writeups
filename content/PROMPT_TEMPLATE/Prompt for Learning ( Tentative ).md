Explain [TOPIC] to someone who has never heard of it before.

Follow this structure:

1. **Hook (1 sentence)** — Start from a problem or situation everyone has experienced in daily life. NOT from a definition or history.

2. **Core problem (2-3 sentences)** — Explain *why* this thing exists / why it matters, zero jargon. If a technical term is unavoidable, define it in the same sentence.

3. **Main analogy** — Use 1 analogy from something hyper-familiar: food, shopping, waiting in line, texting, etc. Avoid analogies from tech / science / business worlds unless you're certain that's your audience.

4. **Concrete breakdown** — Split into 3 simple points. Each point follows this format: [what happens] → [why it matters to the reader].

5. **Sanity check sentence** — End with: "So basically, [TOPIC] is like ____ but for ____." If you can't fill this in simply, the explanation isn't done yet.

⚠️ IMPORTANT — Read before writing:

The examples below are illustrative only. Do NOT copy the tone, wording, or structure verbatim. They exist so you understand the *spirit* of what "good" looks like vs. what "bad" looks like. Apply the same intent to [TOPIC].

❌ BAD EXAMPLE (using "encryption" as the topic):

> "Encryption is a cryptographic method that transforms plaintext into ciphertext using an algorithm and a key, ensuring that only authorized parties can decrypt and access the original data. It is widely used in SSL/TLS protocols and end-to-end messaging applications."

Why it fails:
- Opens with a definition, not a situation
- "Ciphertext", "algorithm", "SSL/TLS" — all jargon with no bridge
- Reader has no reason to care

✅ GOOD EXAMPLE (same topic):

> Ever sent a message and hoped nobody else could read it? That's exactly the problem encryption solves — it scrambles your message into gibberish the moment you hit send, and only unscrambles it when it reaches the right person. Think of it like a lockbox: you and your friend both have the only key, and anyone who intercepts it mid-delivery just sees a locked box they can't open. So basically, encryption is like a secret language but for your digital life.

Why it works:
- Starts with a feeling everyone has had
- Analogy (lockbox) needs zero background knowledge
- Sanity check lands clean

Additional rules:
- Max 3 sentences per paragraph
- If you feel the urge to use "however", "furthermore", or "in other words" more than once → the sentence is too long, break it up
- Never open with history or origin of the topic
